Solar System

The Solar System was formed 4.6 billion years ago. The vast majority of the system's mass is in the Sun, with most of the remaining mass contained in Jupiter. It presents 8 planets around the sun, being Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars with a solid surface and Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune in the gaseous state and Pluto as a dwarf planet.

The solar system has other interesting points, like a moon around the earth in which it has a strong influence on the tides and waves in the oceans. On Saturn, you can see a cloud made up of rocks and ring-shaped particles that orbit the planet.
